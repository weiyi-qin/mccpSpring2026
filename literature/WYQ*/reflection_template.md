# My Reflection on Writing

## Writing Challenges and Difficulties

**What aspects of academic writing do you find most challenging?**

My biggest challenge is the logical flow of storytelling in academic writing. I often struggle with creating a coherent narrative that guides readers smoothly from the problem statement to the proposed solution. Specifically, I find it difficult to:

- Build a compelling "story arc" that connects the research background, the identified gap, and my contribution
- Balance technical rigor with readability and narrative flow
- Transition smoothly between different ideas and sections
- Maintain consistent logic when presenting complex mathematical concepts

**What specific difficulties do you face when writing Introduction/Literature Review?**

For the **Introduction**, I struggle with:
- Identifying and articulating the research gap in a clear and convincing way
- Connecting the motivation (why this problem matters) with the technical contribution (what we propose)
- Deciding how much technical detail to include without overwhelming readers

For the **Literature Review**, I find it challenging to:
- Organize multiple related works into a coherent structure that tells a story rather than just listing papers
- Synthesizing findings from different sources and highlighting connections/contrasts
- Critically evaluating existing work while remaining fair and objective
- Clearly positioning my work relative to existing approaches

**What do you struggle with most?**

My biggest struggle is maintaining the logical flow of the narrative throughout the paper. I sometimes feel that I can explain individual components well, but connecting them into a unified story is difficult. I also worry about whether readers (especially those not deeply familiar with the specific subfield) can follow the logical progression of my arguments.

## My Writing Process

**How do you approach writing Introduction and Literature Review?**

My writing process typically follows these stages:

1. **Define the writing structure/思路**: I start by outlining the main storyline - what is the problem, why is it important, what has been done, what's missing, and what we propose. I organize key points into a hierarchical structure.

2. **Write the first draft/初版**: Once I have a clear structure, I write a complete first version. I focus on getting all the ideas down without worrying too much about perfect phrasing. At this stage, I concentrate on technical accuracy and completeness.

3. **Revise and refine/修改**: I then go through multiple rounds of revision, focusing on:
   - Improving logical flow and transitions
   - Enhancing clarity and readability
   - Ensuring consistency in terminology and notation
   - Polishing language and sentence structure

**What steps do you take?**

My typical workflow:
1. Read and organize relevant literature, taking notes
2. Create a detailed outline with key points for each section
3. Write the first draft section by section (often starting with the Literature Review, then Introduction)
4. Self-review for technical accuracy and completeness
5. Use AI tools to polish language and improve sentence structure
6. Review again for logical flow and storytelling
7. Iterate multiple times before sharing with advisor/collaborators

**Do you have a particular method or strategy?**

Yes, I use a few strategies:
- **Reverse outlining**: After writing a draft, I go back and outline what I actually wrote to check if the structure matches my intended logic
- **"So what?" test**: For each paragraph, I ask myself "why should the reader care?" to ensure I'm maintaining the narrative thread
- **Comparative reading**: I frequently refer to well-written papers in top venues (NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR) to learn how they structure their introduction and related work sections

## How I Use AI for Help

**Do you use AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) to help with writing?**

Yes, I use AI tools extensively, primarily:
- ChatGPT (GPT-4)
- Claude (via Cursor IDE)
- DeepL Write (for language polishing)

**How do you use them?**

I mainly use AI tools for **language polishing and refinement**:
- Improving sentence structure and readability
- Finding more precise academic vocabulary
- Checking grammar and fixing awkward phrasing
- Suggesting alternative ways to express the same idea
- Making technical writing more concise

I typically do NOT use AI for:
- Generating technical content from scratch (I write the technical parts myself)
- Making claims about my research contributions
- Writing mathematical proofs or derivations

**What prompts do you typically use?**

Here are some example prompts I use:

For polishing:
- "Please improve the academic writing quality of this paragraph while maintaining technical accuracy"
- "Make this sentence more concise and clear"
- "Suggest a better transition between these two paragraphs"

For specific improvements:
- "This sentence feels awkward. Can you rephrase it in a more natural academic style?"
- "Help me find a more precise word to describe [concept]"
- "Check if this paragraph has good logical flow"

For readability:
- "Is this explanation clear for readers not familiar with [specific concept]?"
- "Simplify this sentence without losing technical precision"

**What do you find helpful or not helpful about AI assistance?**

**Helpful aspects:**
- AI is excellent at suggesting more natural phrasing and improving sentence structure
- It helps me see alternative ways to express ideas that I might not have thought of
- It's fast and available 24/7, which is convenient during intensive writing periods
- It can catch grammatical errors and awkward expressions that I might miss

**Not helpful aspects:**
- AI sometimes makes suggestions that are grammatically correct but technically imprecise or change the intended meaning
- It can occasionally introduce overly complex vocabulary that sounds unnatural
- AI lacks domain-specific knowledge, so I need to carefully verify any technical content it suggests
- Over-reliance on AI might prevent me from developing my own writing voice and skills

**My approach**: I use AI as a writing assistant and editor, not as a ghostwriter. I always write the first draft myself, then use AI to polish and refine specific parts. I critically evaluate every AI suggestion before accepting it.

## My Goals

**What do you hope to improve in your writing?**

My primary goals are:

1. **Master the art of storytelling in technical writing**: I want to develop the ability to craft a compelling narrative that makes readers care about my research problem and understand why my solution is important.

2. **Develop a reviewer's perspective**: I want to train myself to read my own writing as a critical reviewer would, anticipating questions and objections, and addressing them proactively in the paper.

3. **Improve synthesis skills**: I want to get better at synthesizing multiple sources in the literature review, showing connections and contrasts rather than just summarizing papers individually.

4. **Enhance clarity and conciseness**: I want to express complex technical ideas more clearly and concisely, eliminating unnecessary words while maintaining precision.

**What specific skills do you want to develop?**

Specific skills I'm working on:

1. **Gap identification and articulation**: Learn to identify meaningful research gaps and articulate them convincingly
2. **Critical analysis**: Develop the ability to critique existing work constructively and position my contribution effectively
3. **Logical transitions**: Improve connecting different ideas smoothly with better transition sentences and paragraphs
4. **Academic voice**: Develop a confident, authoritative academic writing style
5. **Self-editing**: Get better at catching logical gaps, unclear explanations, and weak arguments in my own writing
6. **Reviewer mindset**: Learn to anticipate reviewer questions and address potential concerns preemptively

Over time, I hope to internalize good writing patterns so I become less dependent on AI for basic polishing.

When I read excellent papers, I notice they have:
- A clear and compelling story that makes me want to keep reading
- Smooth logical flow where each paragraph naturally leads to the next
- The right balance of high-level intuition and technical depth
- Critical analysis that positions the work clearly in the research landscape

These are the qualities I aspire to achieve in my own writing.
